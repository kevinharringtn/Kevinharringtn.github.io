ROASTING

An exploration of how we turn fruit into drinkable coffee and how each roast is different

There are three distinct ways to roast coffee!

Whether you drink decaf, hot, iced, black, espresso or any other style of coffee, the roast makes a big difference!

Light Roast

Light Roasts carry the most caffeine out of any roast, and the lightest flavor. Because the beans are roasted for the shortest amount of time, they carry a lot of the fruity flavor and acidity that the coffee cherry originally has when it is on the tree. Try out my favorite Light Roast!

Medium Roast

Medium Roasts are roasted a little bit longer than Light Roasts. Because of this they have a richer flavor, with more of a flavor of caramel, toasted nuts and spice coming through than in Light Roasts. However, they have less caffeine than Light Roasts because they are roasted at a high heat for a longer period of time. Try Out My Favorite Medium Roast!

Dark Roast

Dark Roast is the most infamous and strongest tasting cup of coffee. However, it has the least caffeine. This is because most of the caffeine is roasted away. Dark Roast is often used for espresso. Try Out My Favorite Dark Roast!
